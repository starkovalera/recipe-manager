# Recipe Detail Prototype — Visual Review

Status: Proposed for user approval  
Reviewed: 2026-07-22

## Scope

This review evaluates hierarchy and density only. The prototype is intentionally grayscale and schematic; it is not a color, type, component, icon, or imagery direction.

## Passed findings

- The recipe title remains the strongest element on normal, long-title, and no-cover states.
- The compact cover treatment supports recognition without becoming a hero.
- The approved B2 row leads the secondary group without competing with the title or primary action.
- Collections and Tags remain bounded to complete visible names plus `+N` disclosure.
- Ingredients and Instructions read as two continuous semantic columns rather than repeated cards.
- Estimated Nutrition and Cooking Notes remain subordinate to the two primary content streams.
- The review status is visible but does not transfer warning semantics into the neutral Import Info action.
- Information density is product-oriented rather than decorative or recipe-blog-like.

## Risks and required follow-up

- The prototype uses borders heavily to make low-fidelity regions inspectable. These borders must not become a card-heavy visual system.
- Long titles create a tall but stable identity region. High fidelity must use type scale and spacing—not smaller unreadable text—to manage this state.
- Dense mobile metadata creates several horizontal rules in succession. The visual phase must clarify grouping without turning every value into a chip or card.
- Media placeholders are deliberately neutral. No conclusions about photography ratios, cropping, or thumbnail hierarchy can be approved from this iteration.
- Evaluation controls are excluded from screenshots and are not a proposal for product navigation.

## Visual verdict

The hierarchy is strong enough to proceed to a visual-direction phase after the remaining responsive media decision. No polished visual treatment is approved yet.
